/ The challenge

A unique product narrative locked behind paid social and zero-intent traffic.

Aretto possessed a stellar product hook: patented expandable kids' shoes ("world's first") designed to adapt to a child's growing feet up to 18mm/3 sizes. However, customer discovery depended entirely on expensive paid social campaigns and volatile spikes from their Shark Tank India appearance.

Organic search was confined to branded queries, and Aretto lacked a structured AI-search strategy. Generative engines (ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity) did not recommend the brand for generic category queries like "best kids shoes India" or "barefoot shoes for toddlers" because the brand's entity facts were unverified, unstructured, and fragmented across the web.

/ The Strategy — The 4-Layer Flywheel & E-E-A-T

Leveraging Google's E-E-A-T framework for Generative Search

Generative LLMs do not recommend brands blindly; they look for independent consensus and credible signals. By matching Google's **E-E-A-T** (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness) criteria, we constructed a citation flywheel. We ensured Aretto was backed by qualified pediatric orthopedic expertise, verified by third-party publisher consensus, corroborated by marketplaces, and crawled cleanly by search bots.

01

Entity & Expertise Layer (E-E-A-T)

Formulated a consistent, falsifiable set of entity facts ("Supergrooves™ sole", "expands 18mm / 3 sizes", "zero-drop, wide toe box"). We backed this content with veterinary-approved child podiatrist guides to secure high expertise weight in LLM indexing.

02

Owned Content (AEO)

Built answer-first blog architecture targeting high commercial-intent queries. Restructured FAQs into extractable Q&A blocks and published comparison guides naming competitors so pages are classified as neutral editorial roundups by retrieval systems.

03

Earned Consensus (GEO)

Leveraged Aretto's Shark Tank India S3 appearance and funding rounds (₹4.5Cr with celebrity investor Hardik Pandya) to trigger cross-domain news syndication (Inc42, YourStory). This established the independent consensus LLMs require.

04

Retailer & Technical SEO

Optimized FirstCry brand storefronts to serve as citation proxies. Deployed JSON-LD (Product, FAQ, Org) schemas site-wide, fixed Core Web Vitals (LCP < 2.5s), and ensured crawlers like GPTBot and PerplexityBot had full read access.

TECHNICAL

JSON-LD Schema & Crawler Access

Injected product schema matching properties (brand name, age range, materials) to ensure machine readability. Set robots.txt to explicitly permit GPTBot, PerplexityBot, and Google-Extended, while optimizing Core Web Vitals to keep LCP below 2.5s.

/ The Measurement System

Tracking citation Share of Voice (SoV) manually.

AI referral metrics are notoriously difficult to track in standard analytics. We built a custom **25-Prompt Panel** mimicking real buyer queries (e.g., "best barefoot toddler shoes India", "shoes that expand with kids feet"). Weekly, these 25 queries were run manually across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, and Google AI Overviews to record mention rates, citation links, list position, and competitor mentions.

Tracked Metrics

  • Mention Rate: % of queries where Aretto is listed.
  • Citation Rate: % of queries containing wearetto.com or FirstCry links.
  • Avg List Position: Spot in lists (1st, 2nd, etc.) recommended.
  • Share of Voice (SoV): Aretto mentions / total panel mentions.

Sample Panel Queries

  • "best toddler shoes for foot development India"
  • "shoes that grow with kids feet brand"
  • "patented flexible sole children shoes"
  • "Aretto kids shoes reviews and fit"

/ Key Learnings & Interview Talking Points

1. Mentions ≠ Citations

Generative engines frequently list consumer brands in direct answer texts without placing citation links. Successful programs track mentions and link citations independently to measure overall value.

2. Platform-Specific Playbooks

Only ~11% of domains are cited by both ChatGPT and Perplexity. We ran platform-specific playbooks: entity and earned media work for ChatGPT (parametric), and fresh answer-formatted pages for Perplexity (real-time).

3. The Retailer Storefront Proxy

LLM engines often cite established marketplace links (like FirstCry) up to 4x more than direct D2C product detail pages. Building the identical entity facts directly into Aretto's marketplace store pages bypassed direct PDP authority gates.

4. Earned Media is the True Moat

An optimized Shark Tank India S3 appearance and Pand Pandya news wire created a cross-domain facts consensus. No amount of direct owned content could replicate this depth of LLM trust.
