/ 01 Starting Context

A well-loved local brand losing the search war to platforms it should dominate.

Stock Market Kerala had built real trust on the ground — 3 branches (Kochi, Kozhikode, Thiruvananthapuram), 7+ years operating, 4.7★ reviews on Justdial, and a premium course portfolio. But online, the brand was nearly invisible outside its own name.

When prospective students searched "best stock market course Kerala", "stock market training Kochi", or asked ChatGPT to recommend trading institutes in Kerala — SMK rarely appeared. Entri App, Trade Talks, and TradeMax Academy dominated those conversations. The site's SEO was almost entirely conversion-page focused: great at capturing people who already knew SMK, useless at earning the people who didn't yet.

/ 02 Baseline Audit

What the diagnostic revealed before any work began.

Search visibility gaps:

  • Ranking for brand queries only — zero visibility for non-brand commercial terms.
  • Competitor Entri App ranking top-3 for 80%+ of high-intent Kerala trading course queries.
  • No featured snippet eligibility — zero answer-formatted content anywhere on site.
  • People Also Ask captured by Trade Talks and finfitedu.com — SMK absent.
  • Knowledge Graph entry missing or incomplete — AI engines couldn't resolve the entity.

Content & technical gaps:

  • Only 3 blog articles published — none structured for AI extractability.
  • No FAQ schema, no HowTo schema, no Course schema on course pages.
  • Meta titles missing trust signals — no student count, years of experience, or social proof.
  • Image ALT text generic or absent.
  • Core Web Vitals flagged — LCP not within 2.5s target on mobile.
  • No instructor bios, credentials, or author pages — critical E-E-A-T gap.
/ 03 Strategy

The 4-Layer Organic Authority Engine.

AI engines and Google both operate on the same signal: they cite brands whose entity facts are consistent, whose content answers questions directly, and whose authority is corroborated by independent third-party sources. For SMK, the winning system required four compounding layers.

Layer 01 — Entity

Lock the brand entity facts

One set of verifiable facts — "7+ years operating", "3 branches: Kochi, Kozhikode, Trivandrum", "NISM-trained instructors", "1,000+ students" — repeated consistently across the site, GBP, directories, and all published content.

Layer 02 — Content

Build an answer-first content hub

100+ educational articles written in direct Q→A format targeting every query a Kerala trading student would ask — from "how does a stock market work" through to "best options strategy for beginners India". Each structured for Featured Snippet and AI Overview eligibility.

Layer 03 — Earned Media

Build cross-domain consensus

Secured SMK features and citations on WealthNote, Justdial editorial roundups, regional finance YouTube channels, and Kerala education directories — creating the multi-domain corroboration that makes AI engines confident to recommend a brand.

Layer 04 — Technical

Make every page machine-readable

Deployed Organization + LocalBusiness + Course + FAQPage + BreadcrumbList JSON-LD schemas. Fixed Core Web Vitals LCP to under 2.5s. Enabled GPTBot and PerplexityBot in robots.txt. Implemented self-referencing canonical tags across all indexable pages.

/ 04 Execution

What I Executed

Content Hub — 100+ Article Topical Authority Build Mapped and created content across 5 topical clusters: Beginner Guides, Technical Analysis, Options & Derivatives, Risk Management, and Trading Psychology. Each article was written with a direct answer in the first 2 sentences (targeting the 44% of AI citations that come from a page's first 30%), structured under keyword-rich H2/H3 headers, and published with FAQPage JSON-LD blocks.
SEO On-Page — Meta, Heading & Conversion Page Overhaul Rewrote all course page meta titles to include trust signals: *"Kerala's #1 Stock Market Training Institute — 1,000+ Students | SMK"*. Built 3 city-specific landing pages (Kochi, Kozhikode, Thiruvananthapuram) with locally-optimized content capturing location-intent searches. Added comparison pages targeting commercial-research queries.
E-E-A-T Build — Instructor Authority Pages Built dedicated instructor profile pages featuring NISM certification details, years of market experience, and social proof. Marked up with Person schema so AI engines could attribute subject-matter authority to named individuals.
GEO Work — Cross-Domain Entity Consensus Secured SMK placements in third-party "Best trading institutes Kerala" editorial lists. Each placement carried identical entity facts, building the cross-domain consensus required by AI engines. Optimized SMK's Google Business Profiles for all 3 branches, heavily cited by Gemini and Google AI Overviews.
Technical — Schema Deployment & Core Web Vitals Injected complete JSON-LD schema coverage (Organization, LocalBusiness, Course, FAQPage, BreadcrumbList, Person). Fixed LCP from 4.2s to 2.1s through image compression, lazy loading, and CSS optimization.

/ 08 Key Learnings

Takeaways from the Campaign

  1. Conversion pages are SEO dead ends: A site that's 90% enrollment landing pages is invisible for everything except branded queries. The 100-article content hub was non-negotiable before anything else could move.
  2. Local strength ≠ AI citation strength: SMK had 4.7★ reviews and 3 physical branches — but zero AI presence. AI engines don't crawl Google Maps. They crawl content, structured data, and third-party editorial mentions.
  3. YMYL niche demands named experts: Financial education is a YMYL category. The instructor profile pages with Person schema were among the highest-leverage E-E-A-T investments we made.
  4. GBP is the AI's local entity anchor: Gemini and Google AI Overviews pull heavily from Google Business Profiles for local service recommendations. Optimizing SMK's 3 GBPs was the fastest path to appearing in location-specific AI answers.
/ FAQ

Technical Breakdown: AEO / GEO Methodology

Q: Why does a business appearing in a Perplexity answer matter differently than ranking #1 on Google?

A: Perplexity and ChatGPT don't return a ranked list of links — they synthesize a single answer from a small set of sources they trust enough to cite directly. Being one of the sources an LLM pulls from means the model's retrieval layer judged the page's content as directly answering the query, not just relevant to it. That's a different bar than ranking algorithmically — it's closer to being selected as a citation in a research summary.

Q: The case study distinguishes a "citation" (Perplexity/ChatGPT mentions) from a "voice share estimate." Why does that distinction matter technically?

A: A citation is a direct observation — the engine literally returned it. A voice share estimate is a modeled inference built from proxy signals (Maps prominence, review volume, branded search behavior, social activity) — it approximates visibility across a wider set of unobserved queries, but it's a model output, not a measurement. Treating an estimate as if it were a citation is the single most common error in AEO reporting, and this case study deliberately keeps them separated.

Q: What made Stock Market Kerala's own site content eligible to be pulled into Perplexity's answer in the first place?

A: Perplexity's citation explicitly notes the source "mentions 'offline stock market training' and lists Kochi on its site" — meaning the page's on-page copy contained the literal phrase and location the query was asking about. This reflects a core AEO principle: LLM retrieval favors pages whose language mirrors the way a user actually phrases a question, not just pages that are topically related.

Q: What would need to be true for this position to be defensible against a competitor closing the gap?

A: Sustained content freshness and multi-surface consistency — the same location/service phrasing repeated coherently across the website, directory listings (HiDubai-equivalent), and social presence, since LLMs increasingly cross-reference multiple independent sources rather than trusting a single page.
