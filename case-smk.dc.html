/ Starting context

A well-loved local brand losing the search war to platforms it should dominate.

Stock Market Kerala had built real trust on the ground — 3 branches (Kochi, Kozhikode, Thiruvananthapuram), 7+ years operating, 4.7★ reviews on Justdial, and a premium course portfolio (₹20,000–₹39,500). But online, the brand was nearly invisible outside its own name.

When prospective students searched "best stock market course Kerala", "stock market training Kochi", or asked ChatGPT to recommend trading institutes in Kerala — SMK rarely appeared. Entri App, Trade Talks, and TradeMax Academy dominated those conversations. The site's SEO was almost entirely conversion-page focused: great at capturing people who already knew SMK, useless at earning the people who didn't yet.

/ Strategy

The 4-Layer Organic Authority Engine.

AI engines and Google both operate on the same signal: they cite brands whose entity facts are consistent, whose content answers questions directly, and whose authority is corroborated by independent third-party sources. For SMK, the winning system required four compounding layers.

LAYER 01 — ENTITY
Lock the brand entity facts

One set of verifiable facts — "7+ years operating", "3 branches: Kochi, Kozhikode, Trivandrum", "NISM-trained instructors", "1,000+ students" — repeated consistently across the site, GBP, directories, and all published content.

LAYER 02 — CONTENT
Build an answer-first content hub

100+ educational articles written in direct Q→A format targeting every query a Kerala trading student would ask — from "how does a stock market work" through to "best options strategy for beginners India". Each structured for Featured Snippet and AI Overview eligibility.

LAYER 03 — EARNED MEDIA
Build cross-domain consensus

Secured SMK features and citations on WealthNote, Justdial editorial roundups, regional finance YouTube channels, and Kerala education directories — creating the multi-domain corroboration that makes AI engines confident to recommend a brand.

LAYER 04 — TECHNICAL
Make every page machine-readable

Deployed Organization + LocalBusiness + Course + FAQPage + BreadcrumbList JSON-LD schemas. Fixed Core Web Vitals LCP to under 2.5s. Enabled GPTBot and PerplexityBot in robots.txt. Implemented self-referencing canonical tags across all indexable pages.

/ What I Executed
Content Hub

100+ Article Topical Authority Build

Mapped and created content across 5 topical clusters: Beginner Guides, Technical Analysis, Options & Derivatives, Risk Management, and Trading Psychology.

Each article was written with a direct answer in the first 2 sentences (targeting the 44% of AI citations that come from a page's first 30%), structured under keyword-rich H2/H3 headers, and published with FAQPage JSON-LD blocks. Priority was given to listicle and comparison formats, the most cited in AI commercial recommendations.

Meta, Heading & Conversion Page Overhaul

E-E-A-T Build

Published student case study articles — named alumni success stories with trading milestones and before/after skill transformations, serving as E-E-A-T Experience evidence for search engines evaluating the site's credibility in a YMYL niche.

GEO Work

Cross-Domain Entity Consensus & Citations

Secured SMK placements in third-party "Best trading institutes Kerala" editorial lists, Justdial category roundups, and regional finance education directories. Each placement carried identical entity facts, building the cross-domain consensus required by AI engines.

Optimized SMK's Google Business Profiles for all 3 branches by adding Q&A content mirroring common queries and updated photos with keyword-rich captions. GBP listings are heavily cited by Gemini and Google AI Overviews.

Technical

Schema Deployment & Core Web Vitals Fix

Injected complete JSON-LD schema coverage: Organization, LocalBusiness, Course, FAQPage, BreadcrumbList, and Person schema. Fixed LCP from 4.2s → 2.1s through image compression, lazy loading, and Elementor CSS optimization. Updated robots.txt to explicitly permit GPTBot, PerplexityBot, Google-Extended, and ClaudeBot.

/ Key Learnings
01 — Conversion pages are SEO dead ends

A site that's 90% enrollment landing pages is invisible for everything except branded queries. AI engines and Google's informational ranking both require content that answers questions first — sell second. The 100-article content hub was non-negotiable before anything else could move.

02 — Local strength ≠ AI citation strength

SMK had 4.7★ reviews and 3 physical branches — but zero AI presence. AI engines don't crawl Google Maps. They crawl content, structured data, and third-party editorial mentions. Local SEO and AI search require completely different execution tracks.

03 — YMYL niche demands named experts

Financial education is a YMYL category — Google and LLMs apply extra scrutiny. A faceless institute can't compete against named, credentialed instructors with published content. The instructor profile pages with Person schema were among the highest-leverage E-E-A-T investments we made.

04 — GBP is the AI's local entity anchor

Gemini and Google AI Overviews pull heavily from Google Business Profiles for local service recommendations. Optimizing SMK's 3 GBPs — photos, Q&A, review responses, accurate NAP — was the fastest path to appearing in location-specific AI answers while the content hub built its authority.
